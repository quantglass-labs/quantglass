Reading a Candlestick

A beginner-friendly, visual trading lesson that explains how one candle summarizes the battle between buyers and sellers.

Estimated time: 5–7 min Key terms: OHLC, body, wick, rejection Mission unlock: Read 10 Candles Like a Trader

Lesson Promise

By the end of this lesson, you will understand what one candlestick tells you, what it does not tell you, and why a candle must always be read with context.

Main idea: A candlestick is a price story, not a guaranteed signal.

1. What Is a Candlestick?

A candlestick is a visual summary of price movement during one selected time period.

Candle timeframeWhat one candle represents
1 minuteAll price movement during 1 minute
1 hourAll price movement during 1 hour
1 dayAll price movement during 1 trading day
1 weekAll price movement during 1 week

A candle answers four questions: where price started, how high it went, how low it went, and where it finished. These are called OHLC.

O = Open
H = High
L = Low
C = Close

3. Green Candle vs Red Candle

A candle is usually shown as green when price closes above its open. It is usually shown as red when price closes below its open.

Beginner warning: Green does not automatically mean buy. Red does not automatically mean sell.

4. From Ticks to One Candle

Every small trade is a tick. A candle groups many ticks into one visual summary.

5. Worked Example

Imagine a candle has this data:

OHLC valuePrice
Open$100
High$110
Low$95
Close$104

The candle is green because the close is above the open.

Close = $104
Open  = $100

$104 > $100
Result: Green candle

The upper wick shows price reached $110 but could not stay there. The lower wick shows price dropped to $95 but recovered.

6. Candle Body: Strength of the Final Move

Body typeSimple meaning
Large green bodyBuyers strongly pushed price higher
Large red bodySellers strongly pushed price lower
Small bodyNeither side clearly won
Tiny body with long wicksBoth sides fought, but neither fully controlled the candle

7. Wicks: Where Price Was Rejected

A wick shows that price visited an area but could not stay there.

Long Upper Wick

Price moved up, then sellers pushed it back down. Higher prices were rejected.

Stronger when: it appears near resistance.

Long Lower Wick

Price moved down, then buyers pushed it back up. Lower prices were rejected.

Stronger when: it appears near support.

8. Location Matters More Than Shape

The same candle can mean different things depending on where it appears.

9. Context Matrix

10. Candle Reading Checklist

  1. Is it green or red?
  2. Is the body large or small?
  3. Are the wicks long or short?
  4. Is the candle near support?
  5. Is the candle near resistance?
  6. Is it with or against the trend?
  7. Is volume high or low?
  8. Does the candle fit a valid setup?
  9. Where would the trade idea be invalid?
  10. What is the risk before any possible reward?

11. Common Beginner Mistakes

MistakeWhy it is dangerous
Buying every green candleGreen does not guarantee the next candle will rise
Selling every red candleRed does not guarantee continuation lower
Reading one candle aloneContext matters more than the shape
Ignoring support and resistanceLocation changes meaning
Ignoring volumeWeak participation can weaken the candle message
Forgetting risk managementEven a good setup can fail

12. One Candle Is Not a Signal

A candle is only one piece of information. A better decision combines candle, trend, support/resistance, volume, and risk.

13. Self-Check Quiz

Question 1: What does OHLC stand for?

Answer: Open, High, Low, Close.

Question 2: A candle opens at $30 and closes at $35. What color is it usually?

Answer: Green, because close is above open.

Question 3: What does a long upper wick usually show?

Answer: Price moved higher but was rejected from higher levels.

Question 4: Why should you not trade from one candle alone?

Answer: Because context such as trend, support, resistance, volume, and risk matters.

14. Practice Task

Look at five candles on a sample chart. For each candle, write:

1. Is it green or red?
2. Is the body large or small?
3. Is there a long upper wick?
4. Is there a long lower wick?
5. Is the candle near support or resistance?
6. What might the candle be showing?
7. Would you need more context before trading?

Final Takeaway

A candlestick shows where price started, how far buyers and sellers pushed, where price was rejected, and who controlled the close.

A good trader does not ask only: “Is this candle green?”
A good trader asks: “What is this candle showing, where did it appear, and does it fit a valid plan?”
