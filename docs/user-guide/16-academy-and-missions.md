# The Academy, Missions, and the Feedback Loop

QuantGlass v0.2.0 added a complete trading education layer. This page maps
the six areas and how they connect. Everything is educational — never
financial advice — and everything runs locally.

## Learn (the Academy)

121 lessons across 23 tracks and four levels (novice → intermediate →
advanced → expert). Each lesson has a concept, key terms, an exercise, and
often interactive visuals or a live exercise computed from your own stored
market data. The sidebar buttons open:

- **Glossary** — every key term across the catalog, searchable, each
  linking back to its lesson.
- **Reference** — indicators with formulas, order types, core risk
  formulas, and the scam red-flag checklist.
- **Practice** — spaced-repetition flashcards over lessons you completed,
  plus XP, your daily streak, and per-track badges.
- **Progress** — per-level/per-track completion, weekly pace, and local
  completion certificates (all lessons + the level exam earn one).

Levels unlock through the **readiness ladder**: lessons (or test-out via
the exam), exams, paper-trade counts, and conduct missions. If a model is
configured in Settings → AI, every lesson has an **Ask the Tutor** box
grounded in that lesson's content.

## Missions

A catalog of 108 behavioral certifications across 12 categories, earned by
conduct, not clicks — every criterion reads your real activity. Accept up
to three at a time; active missions pin your next objective with a link to
where you act on it. Most missions lead with a **decision drill**: a short
interactive scenario scored on Process / Risk / Discipline where severe
choices (no stop, averaging down, all-in) fail the run regardless of score.
**Replay missions** play stylized market episodes bar-by-bar with graded
debriefs.

## Paper → Journal → Review (the feedback loop)

1. **Capture**: the paper ticket requires a plan — stop, target, risk %,
   written reason, emotional state.
2. **Score**: every executed trade gets a process score (0–100) and, once
   resolved, lands in the 2×2: earned win, well-played loss, honest
   tuition, or **dangerous success** (bad process rewarded — the habit
   that bills accounts later).
3. **Journal**: add reflection notes and mistake tags to each trade.
4. **Review**: weekly process metrics, the quadrants, repeated-mistake
   detection with the exact lessons and missions that train each fix, an
   AI coach narrative (fact-guarded), and your **Trading Constitution**.

## The Trading Constitution

Your own rules — max risk per trade, daily trade cap, stop and
written-reason requirements — adopted in Review and **enforced at the
ticket**: violating submissions are rejected with the broken rules named.
Amend it on scheduled reviews, never mid-impulse.

## How it gates

Real-money trading stays off by default. The ladder is deliberately
conduct-based: advanced requires the Risk Discipline mission, expert
requires The Operator, and live-trading confirmation additionally requires
an OS keychain for credentials. The product's stance: prove the behavior
on paper first.
